# EPPP memory-aid review wave 03

Status: source and editorial review complete; independent qualified expert review and production validation pending.

This balanced wave rewrites 16 additional high-risk legacy aids - two per EPPP domain. Stable legacy IDs prevent same-title duplicates from being promoted accidentally. The review removes neurochemical reductionism, deterministic medication and performance rules, culturally reductive rankings, prescriptive grief stages, mnemonic-only diagnosis, one-size-fits-all treatment matching, psychometrically absolute score claims, and legally universal reporting rules.

| Domain | Memory aid | Editorial outcome | Sources |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Neurotransmitter Functions | Replaced one-transmitter/one-disorder equations with receptor-, pathway-, and circuit-aware functions. | [Neuroscience: Neurotransmitters](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK10795/); [Physiology, Neurotransmitters](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK539894/) |
| 1 | Antipsychotics & Side Effects | Replaced a deterministic timeline and class dichotomy with medication-specific risk recognition and monitoring. | [NICE CG178 recommendations](https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg178/chapter/recommendations); [NIMH: Schizophrenia](https://www.nimh.nih.gov/health/publications/schizophrenia) |

| 3 | Berry's Acculturation Strategies | Removed dominant-culture ownership language, fixed rankings, and person-only explanations of acculturation. | [Immigration, Acculturation, and Adaptation](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1464-0597.1997.tb01087.x); [APA Multicultural Guidelines](https://www.apa.org/about/policy/multicultural-guidelines.pdf) |
