# AlloFlow + University of Maine collaboration packet

**Working draft — July 14, 2026**  
**Status:** Prepared for exploratory discussion. Not reviewed, approved, or endorsed by the University of Maine or the University of Maine System (UMS). Nothing in this packet is legal advice or a commitment by either party.

## Purpose

This packet turns a broad idea — making AlloFlow a durable, open public-interest project with an academic research home — into a sequence that UMaine can evaluate without being asked to endorse the entire platform.

The proposed first collaboration is a bounded study of an AlloFlow workflow for AI-assisted, accessible instructional differentiation. The project would ask whether the workflow helps educators create better differentiated materials with less time and revision burden. It would not begin by claiming that AlloFlow is an evidence-based intervention.

## The recommended sequence

### Phase 1 — Conversation, no transfer

- Ask Dr. Howorth for a 30-minute reaction meeting.
- Demonstrate one workflow, not the entire platform.
- Invite her to reshape or reject the research question.
- Do not ask for endorsement, employment, a grant commitment, or IP transfer in the first conversation.

### Phase 2 — Small feasibility partnership

- Agree on a frozen research build and protocol.
- Complete technical, accessibility, privacy, conflict-of-interest, and IRB review as applicable.
- Pursue seed funding or a small planning award.
- Budget a defined technical role for Aaron if UMaine determines that role is necessary and allowable.
- Use a limited collaboration and stewardship MOU; do not assign the trademark or copyright merely to begin a pilot.

### Phase 3 — Evidence and durable stewardship

- Publish results regardless of whether they favor AlloFlow.
- Use the results to decide whether a larger research, implementation, or statewide proposal is justified.
- If both sides want a permanent home, negotiate joint governance or assignment with enforceable open-source, continuity, and reversion protections.

## Core position

AlloFlow should be offered as a **researchable open infrastructure project**, not donated as the price of admission. UMS can receive broad, durable public-interest rights and meaningful governance without an unconditional transfer on day one. Any eventual assignment should follow independent legal review and include continuity obligations and a reversion mechanism if active stewardship ends.
