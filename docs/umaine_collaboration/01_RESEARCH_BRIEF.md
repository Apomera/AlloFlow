# AlloFlow research collaboration brief

**Working title:** Evaluating AI-assisted accessible differentiation in inclusive classrooms  
**Proposed research lead:** Sarah K. Howorth, Ph.D., BCBA-D, University of Maine — subject to her interest and institutional approval  
**Technology and implementation lead:** Aaron Pomeranz, Psy.D., AlloFlow creator — proposed role only  
**Version:** Exploratory draft, July 14, 2026

## Opportunity

Educators are increasingly using generative AI to adapt texts, create scaffolds, and produce instructional materials. The speed benefit is plausible, but important questions remain unanswered: Are the materials accessible? Are they instructionally appropriate? How much educator revision is required? Do structured safeguards improve the result? Can this work be done without creating another student-data system?

AlloFlow is an AGPL-licensed, local-first education platform created to support Universal Design for Learning (UDL), accessibility, differentiated instruction, and inclusive participation. Its breadth makes it unsuitable for evaluation as a single intervention. This proposal therefore isolates one reproducible workflow:

> Source material → educator-specified learner needs and instructional goal → differentiated reading and scaffolds → automated checks → educator review and revision → accessible export.

The study would evaluate that workflow, not validate AlloFlow as a whole.

## Proposed research question

For practicing and preservice educators, does a structured AI-assisted UDL workflow, compared with the educator's usual material-development process, affect:

1. preparation time;
2. blinded ratings of accessibility and instructional quality;
3. the number and severity of errors requiring correction;
4. educator cognitive workload, usability, and trust; and
5. the degree and type of human revision before classroom use?

## Study design: two-stage feasibility program

### Stage 1 — Educator crossover study

Approximately 15–30 practicing or preservice educators would each complete two matched material-development tasks: one using their usual process and one using the frozen AlloFlow research workflow. Task order and source materials would be counterbalanced where feasible.

Primary feasibility outcomes:

- elapsed active work time;
- blinded expert rubric score for accessibility and instructional quality;
- critical factual or pedagogical error count;
- revision burden;
- task completion rate; and
- System Usability Scale or another investigator-selected usability measure.

This stage can avoid collecting student records and is not powered or framed as an efficacy trial.

### Stage 2 — Optional classroom feasibility

Only after Stage 1 and required institutional reviews, a small number of educators could use the workflow in authentic classrooms. Outcomes would emphasize feasibility, implementation fidelity, educator adoption, and technical reliability. Student learning outcomes would be exploratory unless a later study is appropriately designed and powered.

## Research safeguards

- Freeze and checksum the research build, prompt templates, model configuration, and scoring rubrics.
- Record model/provider/version and generation metadata without collecting prompt content containing student PII.
- Prohibit names, IEP documents, education records, and identifiable student work in the initial study.
- Use local or institutionally approved services only, as determined by UMaine.
- Predefine adverse-output and stop criteria.
- Preserve investigator independence and publish null or unfavorable findings.
- Treat automated accessibility checks as screening evidence, not certification.
- Complete IRB or a formal determination before recruitment or data collection.

## Deliverables

1. A preregistered or otherwise prospectively documented protocol.
2. A frozen, documented AlloFlow research build.
3. An accessible-material quality rubric and scoring guide.
4. An anonymized or de-identified analysis dataset, if permitted.
5. A technical and feasibility report.
6. A manuscript or conference submission led under normal academic authorship standards.
7. A go/no-go decision for a larger classroom or implementation study.
