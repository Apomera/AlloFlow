# Image shot-list — Figurative Language (Grade 5)

Generate in-app (Image tool) or any generator; attach to the noted resource. Alt text is
written to born-accessible standards — use it verbatim.

## 1. `fl-img-washing-machine` — attach to "The Sideways Sentence" (fl-reading)
**Prompt:** A playful illustration of a nervous student on the first day of school, with a
thought bubble showing a washing machine on spin cycle where her stomach would be. Warm,
kid-friendly, expressive, not scary.
**Alt text:** "A student stands nervously with a backpack on the first day of school. A
thought bubble shows a washing machine spinning fast, representing the churning feeling in her
stomach."

## 2. `fl-img-five-tools` — optional, attach to "Name That Tool" (fl-sort)
**Prompt:** A colorful five-panel poster, one panel per tool — simile, metaphor,
personification, hyperbole, idiom — each with a tiny cartoon of a short example. Bold labels,
flat illustration style.
**Alt text:** "A five-panel poster. Simile: a laugh drawn beside a ringing bell. Metaphor: a
classroom drawn as a zoo. Personification: a door with a face shoving itself shut. Hyperbole: a
backpack drawn as heavy as a boulder. Idiom: a person biting a bullet with a puzzled expression."

## 3. `fl-img-literal-vs-figurative` — optional, attach to the quiz
**Prompt:** A humorous split image: on the left, a literal drawing of cats and dogs falling
from the sky with an umbrella; on the right, ordinary heavy rain. Labeled "what the words say"
and "what it means."
**Alt text:** "A split image. On the left, labeled what the words say, cartoon cats and dogs
fall from the sky. On the right, labeled what it means, ordinary heavy rain falls on a street."
