# Image shot-list — Magnetism (Grade 6-7)

## 1. `mg-img-field-lines` — attach to "The Push You Can Feel Through Nothing" (mg-reading)
**Prompt:** A clean educational illustration of a bar magnet with iron filings revealing curved
field lines arcing from the north pole to the south pole, poles labeled N and S in different
colors. Beside it, a small inset showing two magnets repelling with their like poles facing.
Flat style, high contrast, no photorealism.
**Alt text:** "A bar magnet with north and south poles labeled. Iron filings around it form
curved lines arcing from one pole to the other. An inset shows two magnets with matching poles
facing, pushing apart."

## 2. `mg-img-cut-magnet` — optional, attach to the quiz
**Prompt:** A three-step illustration: one bar magnet labeled N and S; the same magnet cut in
half; then the two halves shown each with their OWN N and S labels. A caption reads "you always
get two complete magnets."
**Alt text:** "Three steps showing a bar magnet labeled north and south, then cut in half, then
each half labeled with its own north and south pole."

## 3. `mg-img-electromagnet` — optional, attach to the anchor chart
**Prompt:** A simple circuit diagram for kids: an iron nail wrapped in coiled wire connected to
a battery, picking up paperclips, with a switch shown in the ON position and a small inset of
the switch OFF and the paperclips falling.
**Alt text:** "An iron nail wrapped in coiled wire is connected to a battery and picks up
paperclips while the switch is on. An inset shows the switch off and the paperclips falling."
