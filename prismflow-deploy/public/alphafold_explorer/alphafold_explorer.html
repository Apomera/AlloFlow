Start here

AlphaFold is an AI system that predicts a protein's 3D shape from its amino acid sequence. AlphaFold DB provides public predicted structures; this explorer helps students inspect those predictions, prepare safe classroom inputs, and write cautious claims from evidence.
  • Load a public example or public UniProt/AlphaFold accession.
  • Inspect shape, domains, pockets, surfaces, flexible regions, confidence, and PAE when available.
  • Use "suggests" or "is consistent with" because a prediction is a model, not final proof.
  • Prepare JSON locally only for public, synthetic, or teacher-approved classroom samples.

Evidence checklist

  • Use pLDDT/confidence to separate stronger regions from uncertain loops or tails.
  • Use PAE when available before making claims about domain positions or interfaces.
  • Treat predicted structures as models until supported by experiments or trusted references.
