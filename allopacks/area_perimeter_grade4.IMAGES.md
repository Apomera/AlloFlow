# Image shot-list — Area and Perimeter (Grade 3-4)

## 1. `ap-img-fence-floor` — attach to "The Fence and the Floor" (ap-reading)
**Prompt:** One rectangular dog yard drawn twice. On the left, the outside edge is traced in a
bold dashed line labeled PERIMETER = the fence, 26 feet. On the right, the same rectangle is
filled with a grid of 1-foot squares labeled AREA = the floor, 40 square feet. A happy dog in
each. Bright, simple, large labels.
**Alt text:** "The same rectangular dog yard shown twice. On the left, a bold dashed line
traces the outside edge labeled perimeter, twenty-six feet. On the right, the inside is filled
with a grid of one-foot squares labeled area, forty square feet."

## 2. `ap-img-same-perimeter` — optional, attach to the quiz
**Prompt:** Two rectangles side by side, both labeled "24 feet of fence": a 6 by 6 square
filled with 36 small squares, and a 2 by 10 rectangle filled with 20 small squares, each
labeled with its area. Caption: same fence, different yard.
**Alt text:** "Two rectangles, both using twenty-four feet of fence. The six-by-six square is
filled with thirty-six unit squares. The two-by-ten rectangle is filled with only twenty unit
squares."
