# Image shot-list — Proportional Relationships (Grade 7)

## 1. `pr-img-three-forms` — attach to "When Doubling One Doubles the Other" (pr-reading)
**Prompt:** A three-panel illustration showing the SAME proportional relationship three ways:
a table with rows (1,3),(2,6),(3,9); the equation y = 3x; and a graph with a straight line
through the origin. A label connects them: 'same relationship, three forms.' Clean and
colorful.
**Alt text:** "Three panels of the same relationship. A table shows one maps to three, two to
six, three to nine. The middle shows the equation y equals three x. The right shows a straight
line rising through the origin on a graph."

## 2. `pr-img-through-origin` — optional, attach to the anchor chart
**Prompt:** Two small graphs side by side. Left: a line through the origin labeled
'proportional'. Right: a line crossing the y-axis at 10, above zero, labeled 'not proportional
— has a starting amount'. Clean, two colors.
**Alt text:** "Two graphs. On the left, a line passes straight through the origin, labeled
proportional. On the right, a line crosses the vertical axis above zero, labeled not
proportional because it has a starting amount."
