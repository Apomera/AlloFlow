# Image shot-list — Linear Equations (Grade 8)

## 1. `le-img-slope-intercept` — attach to "Slope and Start" (le-reading)
**Prompt:** A graph of a line y = x + 10 for 8th graders. Clearly mark the y-intercept where the
line crosses the vertical axis at 10 (labeled 'b = start'), and mark a rise-over-run triangle
along the line (labeled 'm = slope'). Clean grid, bold labels.
**Alt text:** "A graphed line crossing the vertical axis at ten, labeled b equals start. A small
step triangle along the line shows the rise over the run, labeled m equals slope."

## 2. `le-img-three-slopes` — optional, attach to the anchor chart
**Prompt:** Three small graphs in a row: a line rising to the right labeled 'positive slope', a
line falling to the right labeled 'negative slope', and a flat horizontal line labeled 'zero
slope'. Simple, one color each.
**Alt text:** "Three graphs. One line rises to the right, labeled positive slope. One falls to
the right, labeled negative slope. One is flat and horizontal, labeled zero slope."
