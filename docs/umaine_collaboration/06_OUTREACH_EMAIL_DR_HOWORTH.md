# Draft outreach email to Dr. Sarah Howorth

**Subject:** Possible UMaine study: AI-assisted accessible differentiation

Hi Dr. Howorth,

I’m Aaron Pomeranz, a school psychologist and the creator of AlloFlow, a free, AGPL-licensed, local-first platform for accessible differentiation, UDL, and inclusive learning tools.

I’ve followed UMaine’s description of your work on AI in special education, assistive and instructional technology, and inclusive teacher preparation. I think there may be a useful research question at the intersection of that work and one bounded part of AlloFlow—but I’m not looking for an endorsement of the platform.

The question is:

> Compared with educators’ usual process, does a structured AI-assisted UDL workflow help them create accessible differentiated materials with less preparation time and revision burden, without reducing factual or instructional quality?

I have a functioning workflow that can be frozen into a research build. A first study could be educator-only: participants complete matched material-development tasks with their usual process and with the AlloFlow workflow; blinded reviewers score accessibility, instructional quality, errors, and revision burden. That would avoid beginning with student records and would be framed as feasibility research, not an efficacy claim.

AlloFlow is broader than this one workflow, but I would deliberately keep the research scope narrow. I’m also open to the possibility that you would reshape the question substantially or conclude it is not worth pursuing.

Would you be willing to spend 30 minutes looking at a five-minute demonstration and the attached two-page concept brief? There is no request for endorsement, funding, or institutional commitment at this stage—just your candid reaction to whether the question has research value and fits work at UMaine.

Longer term, my goal is for AlloFlow to remain open source and become a durable public-interest project rather than depend on one person. If research supports its value, I would be open to discussing UMaine/UMS stewardship or joint governance and a grant-funded technical role, but I would want those questions handled separately from the scientific evaluation.

Thank you for considering it,

Aaron Pomeranz, Psy.D.  
School Psychologist and AlloFlow Creator
