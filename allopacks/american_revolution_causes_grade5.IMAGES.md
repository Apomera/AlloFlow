# Image shot-list — Causes of the American Revolution (Grade 5)

## 1. `ar-img-timeline-map` — attach to "A Slow Build" (ar-reading)
**Prompt:** An illustrated timeline strip for 5th graders running 1763 to 1775, with small
scene icons above each date: a treaty document, a stamped paper, a crowd on a snowy Boston
street, chests of tea in a harbor, a closed port gate, delegates at a table, and militia on a
green. Warm historical illustration, clear year labels, no graphic violence.
**Alt text:** "An illustrated timeline from 1763 to 1775. Small scenes mark the end of the
French and Indian War, the Stamp Act, the Boston Massacre, the Boston Tea Party, the closing of
Boston's port, the First Continental Congress, and militia gathering at Lexington."

## 2. `ar-img-two-perspectives` — optional, attach to the quiz
**Prompt:** A respectful two-panel illustration: on the left, a British official at a desk with
war-debt ledgers; on the right, a colonial family reading a tax notice by candlelight. Each
panel has a thought bubble with a short reasonable-sounding sentence. Educational, balanced,
non-caricatured.
**Alt text:** "Two panels. On the left a British official sits with ledgers showing war debt.
On the right a colonial family reads a tax notice by candlelight. Each has a thought bubble
giving their side of the argument."
