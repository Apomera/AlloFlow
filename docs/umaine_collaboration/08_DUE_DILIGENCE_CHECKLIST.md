# Institutional due-diligence checklist

Complete this before any IP assignment, sponsored-project agreement, student-facing research, or public claim of UMaine partnership.

## Project identity and provenance

- [ ] Confirm legal name and contact information of the current copyright owner(s).
- [ ] Inventory all contributors and the basis for including their work.
- [ ] Run a dependency, media, font, model, dataset, and license inventory.
- [ ] Identify generated files and deploy mirrors versus canonical source.
- [ ] Confirm that no employer, contractor, school district, or prior sponsor has an ownership claim.
- [ ] Identify any code that cannot be assigned even though it may be redistributed under license.
- [ ] Document GitHub, Codeberg, domain, signing, package, cloud, and social-account control.
- [ ] Search and document federal/state trademark status; do not imply registration without evidence.
- [ ] Remove secrets and personal tokens from repository history and transfer materials.

## Technical readiness

- [ ] Define the single research workflow and disable unrelated modules.
- [ ] Freeze a reproducible build and dependency lock.
- [ ] Document supported browsers/devices and minimum hardware.
- [ ] Produce an architecture and external-service inventory.
- [ ] Complete threat modeling and dependency/security review.
- [ ] Define severity levels, incident response, patch process, and responsible people.
- [ ] Establish backup maintainers and recovery access.
- [ ] Separate verified claims from roadmap and marketing claims.

## Accessibility

- [ ] Conduct keyboard and screen-reader testing on the exact research workflow.
- [ ] Test magnification/reflow, contrast, reduced motion, focus, errors, and export accessibility.
- [ ] Obtain independent human review; automated tests are not certification.
- [ ] Record known barriers and accommodations.
- [ ] Ensure every study document and consent artifact is accessible.

UMS states that its digital content and tools are subject to its Title II/WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility requirements. See [UMS Title II guidance](https://www.maine.edu/equal-opportunity/title-ii-regulations/).

## Research and data

- [ ] Name an eligible UMaine PI and responsible unit.
- [ ] Obtain IRB review or formal determination before recruitment/data collection.
- [ ] Define data controller/steward, location, access list, retention, deletion, and incident path.
- [ ] Complete provider and data-transfer review.
- [ ] Define preregistration/prospective protocol, analysis, missingness, and deviation rules.
- [ ] Establish independent outcome scoring and inter-rater training.
- [ ] Disclose Aaron's creator, employment, and IP interests.
- [ ] Protect investigator publication rights, including unfavorable findings.
- [ ] Determine authorship and acknowledgement under normal scholarly standards—not as consideration for IP.

## Institutional agreement

- [ ] Identify the authorized UMS contracting parties and signatories.
- [ ] Schedule pre-existing/background IP and separately define project IP.
- [ ] Confirm treatment of grant-funded code under sponsor terms and UMS policy.
- [ ] Select collaboration license, joint stewardship, or assignment model.
- [ ] Define trademark quality control and official-project identity.
- [ ] Define governance seats, voting, conflicts, security authority, and deadlock.
- [ ] Define term, termination, transition, abandonment, and successor/reversion rules.
- [ ] State explicitly whether UMS has any maintenance obligation.
- [ ] State explicitly that the agreement does not guarantee funding or employment.
- [ ] Obtain independent legal advice for Aaron before assignment.

## Grant and employment

- [ ] Confirm active solicitation, eligibility, deadline, and institutional limited-submission process.
- [ ] Start UMaine's internal Notice of Intent/routing process on schedule.
- [ ] Have ORA approve salary, fringe, F&A, subaward, participant-support, and cost-share treatment.
- [ ] Define Aaron's work, effort, supervision, deliverables, and appointment duration.
- [ ] Ask HR whether a competitive search or approved waiver is required.
- [ ] Do not condition employment on favorable study findings.
- [ ] Create a continuity plan for expiration or loss of soft-money funding.

## Public communications

- [ ] Use “exploratory discussion” until an agreement is signed.
- [ ] Use “research collaboration” only when authorized by the agreement.
- [ ] Do not use UMaine/UMS names, seals, or logos as endorsements without permission.
- [ ] Do not call AlloFlow evidence-based until an appropriate body of evidence exists.
- [ ] Publish limitations, version information, conflicts, and funding sources.
