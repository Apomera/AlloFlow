# Image shot-list — Simple Machines (Grade 5-6)

## 1. `sk-img-six-machines` — attach to "The Deal" (sk-reading)
**Prompt:** A six-panel poster, one panel per simple machine — lever (crowbar on a fulcrum),
inclined plane (ramp with a box), wedge (axe splitting wood), screw (bolt with the thread
highlighted as a wrapped ramp), wheel and axle (doorknob with its shaft), pulley (rope over a
grooved wheel lifting a crate). Each labeled, flat colorful illustration with force arrows.
**Alt text:** "A six-panel poster of simple machines: a crowbar resting on a fulcrum, a ramp
with a box being pushed up it, an axe splitting wood, a bolt with its spiral thread
highlighted, a doorknob shown with its shaft, and a rope running over a grooved wheel to lift
a crate. Each panel shows arrows for the direction of force."

## 2. `sk-img-the-trade` — optional, attach to the anchor chart
**Prompt:** A clear comparison of lifting a box 1 meter straight up (short distance, big force
arrow) versus pushing it up a 2-meter ramp to the same height (long distance, small force
arrow), with a caption reading "half the force, twice the distance."
**Alt text:** "Two ways to raise the same box one meter. Lifting it straight up shows a short
path with a large force arrow. Pushing it up a two-meter ramp shows a longer path with a small
force arrow, captioned half the force, twice the distance."
