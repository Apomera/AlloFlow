# OpenStax operational rights policy

Last audited: 2026-07-23

AlloFlow treats OpenStax reading content as noncommercial CC BY-NC-SA 4.0
material and follows the additional generative-AI notice displayed on current
OpenStax book pages.

## Enabled

- In-app reading and navigation
- Local display and reading-accessibility controls
- Local read-aloud and immersive reading views
- Print and plain-text download with attribution
- Saving a reference to the chapter in a lesson
- Opening the official source page for the current section

Every mirrored digital page and export must retain the book's required
`Access for free at .../pages/1-introduction` attribution.

## Blocked without written permission

- Extract to Source or other generic source-text pipelines
- Generative summaries, questions, adaptations, translations, or definitions
- Fluency analysis that sends the reference passage to a model
- Lingua Practice and other cross-tool text handoffs
- Document Builder passage or vocabulary handoffs
- Model training, fine-tuning, retrieval ingestion, or dataset creation

The importer fails closed if either the expected CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 license or
the OpenStax generative-AI permission notice is missing from a section page.

## Permission request draft

To: support@openstax.org

Subject: Permission clarification for noncommercial OpenStax accessibility mirror

Hello OpenStax team,

We are developing AlloFlow, a noncommercial education and accessibility
project. We mirror a teacher-reviewed selection of OpenStax textbook chapters
so learners can use local accessibility features such as adjustable typography,
reading rulers, read-aloud, focus views, bookmarks, print, and attributed
plain-text downloads.

We do not use OpenStax content to train or fine-tune models. Every mirrored
section links to its official source, carries the CC BY-NC-SA 4.0 license, and
includes the book's required "Access for free at" attribution. We have also
disabled Extract to Source, generative-AI features, fluency-model analysis, and
cross-tool text handoffs for OpenStax content.

Could you confirm that this noncommercial accessibility mirroring and the
listed non-AI features are acceptable? Separately, would OpenStax consider
granting written permission for narrowly scoped, user-initiated educational AI
features such as translation, simplified reading versions, vocabulary support,
or practice-question generation? If so, please let us know the attribution,
storage, model-provider, or other conditions you would require.

We would be glad to provide a technical data-flow summary or demonstration.

Thank you.
