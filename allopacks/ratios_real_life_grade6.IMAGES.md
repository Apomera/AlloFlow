# Image shot-list — Ratios in Real Life (Grade 6)

## 1. `rt-img-unit-price` — attach to "The Four-Second Trick" (rt-reading)
**Prompt:** A clean illustration of two cereal boxes on a store shelf: a 12-ounce box marked
$3.60 and an 18-ounce box marked $5.76, each with a shelf tag showing the unit price ($0.30
per ounce and $0.32 per ounce). A small callout circles the smaller box as the better buy.
Bright, simple, educational.
**Alt text:** "Two cereal boxes on a shelf. The twelve-ounce box costs three dollars sixty and
its tag reads thirty cents per ounce. The eighteen-ounce box costs five dollars seventy-six and
its tag reads thirty-two cents per ounce. A callout marks the smaller box as the better buy."

## 2. `rt-img-recipe-scaling` — optional, attach to the math problems
**Prompt:** A simple visual of a pancake recipe scaling: 2 cups flour and 3 cups milk on the
left, an arrow labeled "times 3," and 6 cups flour with 9 cups milk on the right, with a note
that the ratio stays 2 to 3.
**Alt text:** "A recipe scaling diagram. On the left, two cups of flour and three cups of milk.
An arrow labeled times three points to six cups of flour and nine cups of milk on the right,
with a note that the ratio remains two to three."
