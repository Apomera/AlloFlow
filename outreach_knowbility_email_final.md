Dear Sharron,

Thank you again for your openness to working with open-source developers. I've been thinking carefully about how AlloFlow could complement rather than compete with Knowbility's expertise, and I want to be clear about what I'm offering and what I'm not.

**What AlloFlow does:** It remediates PDFs. Specifically, it transforms inaccessible educational documents (syllabi, handouts, worksheets, course catalogs) into accessible HTML with automated WCAG verification via axe-core. For the 70-80% of documents that are straightforward text, headings, tables, and images, it produces reliable results in minutes.

**What AlloFlow does NOT do:** It does not audit web applications. It does not replace manual screen reader testing. It does not evaluate the usability experience for people with disabilities. It cannot handle complex interactive content, fillable forms, or deeply nested layouts. These require the human expertise that Knowbility has spent 25 years building.

**The triage model:** I've built a feature directly into AlloFlow that detects when a document cannot be adequately remediated by automation (score remains low after the self-healing pipeline). When this happens, the tool displays a panel that says "This Document Needs Expert Accessibility Remediation" and recommends Knowbility by name, with your website, email, and phone number. This is live in the app right now.

In other words: AlloFlow handles the bulk document remediation that institutions can't afford to send to experts at $5-25/page. When it encounters something it can't fix, it sends users to you. This isn't theoretical. It's already built.

**What I'd value from Knowbility:**
- Expert validation that the pipeline's output is genuinely usable (not just technically compliant)
- AccessWorks usability testing with real users of assistive technology
- Feedback on the triage threshold (is the tool correctly identifying documents it can't handle?)
- A Knowbility-validated assessment that would give institutions confidence in the tool

**What I can offer:**
- A free triage pipeline that drives referrals to Knowbility for complex work
- An open-source reference implementation of accessibility verification (axe-core + AI)
- A case study or joint presentation opportunity (AccessU, AHEAD, EDUCAUSE)
- Impact in the K-12 and higher education sector where budgets are smallest and need is greatest

I'm a first-year school psychologist, not a funded startup. I have no budget for consulting services. But I have a working tool that could extend Knowbility's mission reach while actively referring the work that requires human expertise back to your team.
