Topic: Digital Rights and Accessibility Advocacy
Grade Level: 9-12
Standards: ISTE 7.1, C3 Framework D2.Civ.1-3, Common Core ELA SL.9-12.4

Focus: How students can use technology to identify and address accessibility barriers in their communities, connecting digital rights to civil rights history.

Content Outline:
1. Historical context: ADA (1990) → Section 508 (1998) → WCAG 2.0 (2008) → DOJ Title II Final Rule (2024)
2. The digital equivalent of physical barriers: stairs = no alt text, narrow doors = no keyboard access
3. Case studies: Target lawsuit (2006), Domino's v. Robles (2019), school district settlements
4. Tools for advocacy: AlloFlow Digital Accessibility Lab, Knowbility's AIR rally, community auditing
5. Student action project: Audit a local government/school website and present findings to administration

Key Partnership: Knowbility (knowbility.org) - nonprofit dedicated to creating an inclusive digital world. Founded in 1999. Programs: AccessU (training), AIR (Accessibility Internet Rally), community outreach.

Activities:
- "Know Your Rights" worksheet analyzing ADA Title II requirements for school websites
- Live audit of a local school or government website using the AlloFlow tool
- Draft a constructive letter to site administrators with specific, actionable recommendations
- Create an accessible infographic explaining one accessibility barrier and its solution
- Peer review: Students audit each other's digital projects for accessibility compliance

Assessment: Advocacy portfolio + community presentation to school administration
