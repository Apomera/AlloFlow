# Grant and grant-funded role strategy

**Working draft — funding status must be rechecked before action.** Program dates below were verified from official pages on July 14, 2026.

## What UMS could realistically do

If there is faculty, departmental, and institutional interest, UMaine/UMS could potentially:

- serve as applicant and award recipient for an eligible grant;
- provide a PI and independent research governance;
- route the proposal through UMaine's Office of Research Administration (ORA);
- budget salary, fringe, equipment, participant support, evaluation, accessibility review, and other allowable costs;
- hire Aaron into a fixed-term or soft-money role if approved and funded;
- enter a collaboration, license, assignment, or sponsored-research agreement through authorized offices;
- provide IRB, research compliance, data stewardship, and grant administration;
- convene schools, Maine DOE, MAIER, educators, families, and other partners;
- host or help preserve an official open-source project; and
- support dissemination, professional development, and future proposals.

UMS would not be expected to promise any of these before faculty sponsorship, unit approval, diligence, budget review, and an award. A faculty member cannot personally guarantee institutional acceptance, employment, IP terms, or grant funding.

UMaine ORA is the office authorized to submit proposals, accept awards, and negotiate sponsored-project terms on behalf of UMS. [ORA describes its authority here](https://umaine.edu/ora/home/about-us-2/).

## A plausible role for Aaron

**Working title:** Open Education Technology Research and Implementation Lead  
**Appointment concept:** 1.0 FTE fixed-term, grant-funded/soft-money professional or research staff role; final classification determined by UMS HR.

### Core responsibilities

- maintain the frozen research build and reproducible releases;
- document model, prompt, accessibility, privacy, and deployment behavior;
- implement protocol-approved technical changes;
- support educator onboarding and sites without participating in outcome scoring;
- operate issue, security, and incident-response workflows;
- prepare open-source documentation and contributor processes;
- translate investigator requirements into testable software;
- support dissemination and sustainability planning; and
- train additional maintainers so the project is not dependent on one person.

### Independence boundary

Aaron should not control recruitment, primary outcome ratings, data exclusions, statistical analysis, or publication decisions. His creator role and any employment/IP interest should be disclosed.

### Budget treatment

Ask ORA to calculate salary range, fringe, F&A, and classification. A proposal should include the actual effort needed rather than assume development can be donated. Candidate direct-cost categories include:

- Aaron salary and fringe;
- PI/co-investigator effort;
- research assistant or project coordinator;
- compensated educators, family/learner advisors, and expert raters;
- accessibility and security assessment;
- approved inference/hosting and test hardware;
- travel and school implementation support;
- dissemination and accessible publication; and
- independent evaluation where appropriate.

UMS policy normally requires competitive hiring, but its search-waiver guidance expressly identifies a person named in a grant for unique skills as a possible waiver case, subject to approval. It also recognizes self-funded research faculty and soft-money roles. This creates a path, not an entitlement. See [UMS search-waiver guidance](https://www.maine.edu/apls/apl-xii-a/) and [UMS hiring policy](https://www.maine.edu/board-of-trustees/policy-manual/section-416/).

## Funding ladder

### 1. Institutional/partner planning support

Use small internal, state, CEC, foundation, gift, or contract funding to:

- run participatory design sessions;
- complete IP/provenance and security reviews;
- finalize measures and protocol;
- prepare the frozen research build; and
- collect educator-only feasibility data.

This is the lowest-risk entry because it builds evidence before a large federal promise.

### 2. NSF FINDERS FOUNDRY — strong future fit

NSF FINDERS FOUNDRY funds K–12 educators, technologists, researchers, parents/guardians, and students to co-design AI-era education solutions. Planning awards are up to $50,000; only Planning awardees may later seek Development awards up to $300,000. The May 27, 2026 Planning deadline has passed, and the November 18, 2026 deadline is for eligible Development proposals—not a new entry point. Monitor for the next Planning cycle and ask the program office about future dates. [Official solicitation](https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/nsf-finders-foundry-national-science-foundation-fostering-interdisciplinary/nsf26-507/solicitation).

Best AlloFlow framing: one co-designed accessible differentiation or inclusive STEM challenge, not the whole platform.

### 3. IES/NCSER Technology for Special Education — strongest research-topic fit

This topic supports education technology intended to improve outcomes for learners with or at risk for disabilities, including academic, social/behavioral, adaptive, transition, communication, and study-skill outcomes. Pursue only when a matching active RFA supports the proposed project type and stage. [Official IES topic page](https://ies.ed.gov/funding/research/programs/special-education-research-grants/technology-for-special-education).

Best AlloFlow framing: development/feasibility of one defined intervention after preliminary evidence, with an appropriate research design.

### 4. OSEP technology/media/materials programs — recurring thematic fit

OSEP programs support accessible technology, implementation, digital materials, and personnel capacity for individuals with disabilities. Competitions and priorities change; use the official program page and current notice rather than assuming a prior competition remains open. [Official Department of Education page](https://www.ed.gov/program/educational-technology-media-and-materials-individuals-disabilities).

### 5. NSF TechAccess: AI-Ready America — institutional/statewide possibility

This is not an AlloFlow product grant. It supports one statewide Coordination Hub per jurisdiction at up to $1 million per year for three years, with possible fourth-year transition support. Round 2 lists a required letter of intent due December 15, 2026 and a full proposal due January 15, 2027. UMaine could consider AlloFlow as one implementation resource within a much broader Maine consortium, but only if it is pursuing the statewide hub and selects AlloFlow through its institutional planning. [Official NSF solicitation](https://www.nsf.gov/funding/opportunities/techaccess-ai-ready-america/nsf26-508/solicitation).

### 6. SBIR/STTR — later, only with an eligible business structure

An education-technology small business could pursue future IES or NSF SBIR/STTR opportunities, potentially with UMaine as research partner or subawardee. This is not the most natural route if the long-term goal is university/public stewardship with no commercial venture. The FY2026 IES SBIR deadline has passed.

## Twelve-month seed-project work plan

| Months | Work |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | Institutional diligence, co-design, protocol, IP background schedule, research-build scope |
| 3–4 | Freeze build, complete accessibility/security review, IRB determination, rater training |
| 5–7 | Recruit and conduct educator crossover study |
| 8–9 | Independent scoring, analysis, defect remediation after dataset lock |
| 10–11 | Report/manuscript, community review, sustainability decision |
| 12 | Submit next-stage proposal or publish a documented no-go/redesign decision |

## Decisions to request from UMaine—not promises

1. Is the research question worth faculty time?
2. Which PI and unit would own scientific responsibility?
3. Is there a suitable internal/partner funding mechanism for planning?
4. Would the unit budget a named technical role if external funding is pursued?
5. Which HR appointment category is appropriate?
6. Which office reviews pre-existing IP and AGPL stewardship?
7. Does UMaine want to participate in the December 2026 AI-Ready America round, and if so, is AlloFlow relevant to that much larger plan?
