# Image shot-list — Plate Tectonics (Grade 6)

## 1. `pt-img-boundaries` — attach to "The Man Who Was Laughed At" (pt-reading)
**Prompt:** A three-panel cutaway diagram of plate boundaries for middle schoolers.
Panel 1 divergent: two plates pulling apart with magma rising to form new crust.
Panel 2 convergent: one plate diving beneath another, with a trench and volcanoes above.
Panel 3 transform: two plates sliding past each other with a jagged fault line.
Flat colorful educational illustration with arrows and clear labels.
**Alt text:** "Three cutaway panels of plate boundaries. In the first, two plates pull apart
and magma rises between them to form new crust. In the second, one plate dives beneath
another, creating a trench and volcanoes. In the third, two plates slide sideways past each
other along a jagged fault."

## 2. `pt-img-pangaea-fit` — optional, attach to the timeline
**Prompt:** Two-panel map: on the left, South America and Africa shown fitting together like
puzzle pieces with matching fossil bands drawn across the joint; on the right, the continents
in their present positions with the same fossil bands now split by the Atlantic.
**Alt text:** "Two maps. On the left, South America and Africa fit together like puzzle pieces
with fossil bands running continuously across the join. On the right, the same continents sit
apart in their modern positions with the fossil bands separated by the Atlantic Ocean."
