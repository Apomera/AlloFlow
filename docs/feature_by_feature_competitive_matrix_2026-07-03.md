# AlloFlow Feature-by-Feature Competitive Matrix - 2026-07-03

This document extends COMPETITOR_COMPARISON.md with a granular feature-by-feature pass. It uses FEATURE_INVENTORY.md as the spine, replaces outdated STEM/SEL tool counts with current source-registry rows, and adds newer studio/research/special-education surfaces called out in the July 3 codebase review.

Important caveat: competitor cells are based on public official pages checked on July 3, 2026. A "No public dedicated comparable found" cell means I did not find a public, dedicated feature in the reviewed source set; it does not prove the vendor has no private beta or paid-tier capability.

## Legend

- No public dedicated comparable found: no direct public feature found in the reviewed official pages.

## Highest-Signal Findings

- AlloFlow is not just competing with lesson generators. Its unmatched areas are PDF/accessibility remediation, special education and school-psych workflows, AAC/symbol supports, adaptive-controller access, current STEM/SEL tool breadth, local/provider flexibility, and born-accessible media studios.
- Competitors are strongest where the job is narrower: Brisk for in-browser teacher workflow and writing feedback, Diffit for fast differentiated resources, Curipod for live writing/feedback lessons, SchoolAI for teacher-designed AI Spaces plus Mission Control, Khanmigo for tutor-quality conversation inside the Khan Academy ecosystem, MagicSchool for broad district-ready AI-tool packaging, and Eduaide for polished instructional-material generation.
- The strategic story should be: AlloFlow has more unique capability surface, but needs clearer first-run packaging, readiness tiers, pilots, procurement/security packet polish, and a smaller demo path that lets evaluators understand the breadth without drowning in it.
