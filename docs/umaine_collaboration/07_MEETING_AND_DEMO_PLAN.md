# First meeting and demonstration plan

## Goal

Learn whether the proposed research question is valuable and what would have to change for it to become credible UMaine work. The goal is not to secure an endorsement or settle governance in one meeting.

## Thirty-minute agenda

| Time | Topic |
|---:|---|
| 0–3 min | Aaron's background and the public-interest goal |
| 3–8 min | Five-minute bounded workflow demonstration |
| 8–15 min | Research question, comparison condition, and outcomes |
| 15–23 min | Dr. Howorth's critique and alternative framing |
| 23–27 min | Institutional fit and who else would need to be involved |
| 27–30 min | One small next step or a clear no-go |

## Five-minute demo script

Use one public-domain or researcher-authored source passage. Do not show student information.

1. **Problem (30 seconds):** An educator needs a differentiated passage, vocabulary support, response scaffold, and accessible export.
2. **Inputs (45 seconds):** Show source, instructional goal, and non-identifying support needs.
3. **Generation (60 seconds):** Run the frozen candidate workflow. Name the provider/model and disclose that output requires review.
4. **Checks (60 seconds):** Show factual-source comparison, accessibility checks, reading-level information, and known limits.
5. **Human revision (60 seconds):** Correct one real issue. Demonstrate that the educator remains responsible.
6. **Research instrumentation (45 seconds):** Show version/build identity, timing, error logging, and export.

Do not tour STEM, SEL, BehaviorLens, report writing, AAC, games, PDF remediation, or the full catalog unless asked.

## Questions to ask Dr. Howorth

- Is the educator crossover question sufficiently important and novel to study?
- Should the first population be practicing teachers, preservice teachers, or both?
- Which output domain is strongest: reading adaptation, social narratives, accessible course materials, or something else?
- What comparison condition would be fair and ecologically valid?
- Which outcomes would matter to the field rather than only to a product developer?
- Who should score accessibility and instructional quality?
- What failure would make the project not worth continuing?
- Should MAIER, Maine DOE, another faculty member, an accessibility office, or school partners be involved?
- Is there an appropriate small planning mechanism before a federal proposal?
- If a grant is later pursued, would a named technical/implementation role be institutionally plausible?

## Governance discussion, only if she invites it

Use this sentence:

> My priority is that AlloFlow remain open and outlive me. I’m open to meaningful UMaine/UMS stewardship, including broad rights or eventual assignment, but I would want to start with a pilot and a staged agreement that protects continuity, research independence, and the open-source mission.

Avoid saying “I will give UMS everything.” That skips the institutional diligence needed to determine whether UMS can and wants to accept stewardship.

## Acceptable meeting outcomes

- A co-design follow-up with no promise of funding.
- An introduction to a better-aligned colleague or office.
- A request for a technical/accessibility review.
- A recommendation to narrow or change the workflow.
- A candid no. That prevents a poor-fit proposal and is still useful.
